Motherboard Power Motherboard Power Slot - Provides Power For The Motherboard To Function.


The Motherboard Is One Of The Most Essential Parts Of A Computer System . It Holds Together Many Of The Crucial Components Of A Computer , Including The Central Processing Unit (CPU) , Memory , And Connectors For Input And Output Devices .
CPU Fan Power CPU Fan Power - Provides Power For The CPU Fan To Function .


The CPU Fan Is Located On Top Of A Computer Processor . It Helps To Pull And Blow Hot Air Off The Processor , Helping To Keep It Cooler .
CPU Socket / Slot CPU Socket Or Slot - Comprises Of One Or More Mechanical Components Providing Mechanical And Electrical Connections Between A MicroProcessor And A Printed Circuit Board. (PCB)


CPU Sockets Are Used On The Motherboard , In Desktop And Server Computers.
PCI Slot Peripheral Component Interconnect (PCI) Slot - It Is A Parallel Interface Or Uses Parallel Bus Structure , Which Deals With A Large Amounts Of Data By Splitting Them Up And Sending Them At Low speeds.


Speed Is Measured In Megahertz


It Connects Apparatus For A 32 - Bit Computer Bus.


These Tools Are Built Into The Motherboard Of Computers And Devices In Order To Allow For The Addition Of PCI Devices Like Modems , Network Hardware Or Sound And Video Cards.
PCI Express X1 Slot Peripheral Component Interconnect Express (PCIe) X1 Slot - Replaces The Standard PCI Port Or Slot And Are About 1 Inch (26mm) Long . It Uses Parallel Interface.


It Moves Data On And Off The Motherboard At Up to 250 Megabytes Per Second (MBps). In Each Direction At Once.


One Of The Most Common Uses Of The 1X Slots Are For Port Expansion Cards, Where Users Are Looking To Increase The Number Of Specific Ports On Their Computers.
PCI Express X16 Slot Peripheral Component Interconnect Express (PCIe) x16 Slot - It Is A Serial Interface Unlike The Normal PCI Slots , And Is a Parallel Interface.


In Serial Interface, Data Are Send One At A Time , Really Fast .


PCI Express Interface allows high Bandwidth Communication Between The Device And The Motherboard , As Well As Other Hardware .


PCI Express X16 has 82 Pins And Is 89mm In Length .
Hard Drive Power Hard Drive Power - Provides Power For The Hard Drive To Function And Store Data . The Hard Drive Power Is Also known As A Power Supply .


There Are Various Types Of Power Supply That Provides Power To Internal Hard Disk , Floppy Disk , CD/DVD And Other Drives Directly , Through The Four Wire Connectors That Are Designed To Attach To The Rear Of Each Drive . The Four Wires Provide +5 volt and +12 volt Power , Along With Two Grounds , To The Various Drives That Use Them .
Hard Drive Data (IDE) Hard Drive Data (IDE) , Also Known As Integrated Drive Electronics (IDE) - It Is A Type Of Hard Disk Drive (HDD) , Which Is A Data Storage Device Used For Storing And Retrieving Digital Information Using Rapidly Rotating Disks Coated With Magnetic Material.


Integrated Drive Electronics (IDE) Hard Disks Have Been Around For Quite A Few Years.


It Is A Standard Electronic Interface Used Between A Computer's Motherboard Data Paths Or Bus And The Computer's Disk Storage Devices.


Prior To The IDE Hard Drives , Controllers Were Separate External Devices . They Were Interfaced To A Computer Motherboard Via An Expansion Board known as A Hard Disk Controller . The IDE Reduced Problems Associated With Storage Devices And Integrated Controllers.
Hard Drive Data (SATA) Hard Drive Data (SATA) , Also Known AS Serial ATA (Serial Advanced Technology Attachment Or SATA) - It Is A Standard For Connecting And Transferring Data From Hard Disk Drives (HDDs) To Computer Systems.


It Delivers Point To Point Channel Communication Between The Computer Main Board And Hard Drive .


SATA Works As A Bus , Providing High Speed Data Transfer To And From The Hard Disk Drive , At A Rate Of 1.5 GB/s.


SATA Drives Are Based On Serial Signaling Technology , Unlike Integrated Drive Electronics (IDE) Hard Drives That Use Parallel Signalling .

VGA Port Video Graphics Array (VGA) Connector - Old monitor connector , aka PC - RGB , D - sub 15 , Analog


It is a three - row 15 pin connector . The 15 - pin connector was provided on many video cards , computer monitors , laptop computers , projectors , and high definition television sets . Many small laptop or other devices , a mini VGA port is used instead of the full sized VGA connector .
DVI Port Digital Visual Interface (DVI) - It is a video display interface developed by the Digital Display Working Group (DDWG).


The digital interface is used to connect a video source , such as a video display controller , to a display device , such as a computer monitor
HDMI Port High - Definition Multimedia Interface (HDMI) - It is a proprietary audio/video interface for transmitting uncompressed video data and compressed or uncompressed digital audio data from an HDMI - compliant source device , such as a display controller , to a compatible computer monitor , video projector , digital television or digital audio device.


HDMI is a digital replacement for analog video standards
PS2 Mouse Port PS2 Mouse Port - It is a 6 - pin mini - DIN connector used for connecting keyboards and mice to a PC compatible computer system . A DIN connector is an electrical connector that was originally standardized in the early 1970s.


Its name comes from the IBM Personal System/2 series of personal computer.


It is used for connecting a mouse to a Personal computer compatible computer system.
PS2 Keyboard Port PS2 Keyboard Port - It is a keyboard port which was developed by IBM . It is used to connect a computer keyboard to an IBM compatible computer.


The PS2 port is a mini DIN plug that contains six pins and is still sometimes found on all IBM compatible computers .
USB 2 Port USB 2.0 - It is a Universal Serial Bus (USB) standard . Almost all devices with USB capabilities , and nearly all USB cables , support at least USB 2.0 .


Devices that adhere to the USB 2.0 standard have the ability to transmit data at a maximum speed of 480 Megabits per second (Mbps).
USB 3 Port USB 3.0 - It is the third major version of the Universal Serial Bus (USB) standard for interfacing computers and electronic devices.


There are various improvement in the USB 3.0 such as the new transfer rate referred to as SuperSpeed USB(SS) that can transfer data at up to 5 Gigabits per second(625 MB/s) , which is about 10 times faster than the USB 2.0 standard .
Displayport Displayport - It is a digital display interface developed by the Video Electronics Standards Association (VESA).


The interface is primarily used to connect a video source to a display device such as a computer monitor , and it can also carry audio , USB , and other forms of data .


DisplayPort was designed to replace VGA , DVI , and FPD-Link . The interface is backward compatible with other interfaces such as HDMI and DVI , through the use of either active or passive adapters .
Thunderbolt Port Thunderbolt Port - It is an interconnect technology developed by Intel in cooperation with Apple . Thunderbolt combines PCI Express and DisplayPort into two serial signals , and additionally provides DC power , all in one cable allowing for a combination of up to six peripherals , like storage devices and monitors , to be daisy - chained together .


Thunderbolt 1 and 2 use the same connector as Mini DisplayPort (MDP) , whereas Thunderbolt 3 re-uses the Type-C connector from USB .


It was initially developed and marketed under the name Light Peak , and first sold as a part of a consumer product on 24 February 2011 .
Sound (7.1) Port Sound (7.1) - It gives high quality output on audio , and works well with any computer , device and operating system.


Analog Output.
Sound (Headphone) Port Sound (Headphone/Audio) Port - It is to which an audio device such as speaker , headphones or a microphone can be connected.


An analog audio port
Sound (Microphone) Port Sound (Microphone) Port - It gives an analog output signal .


Microphone is used to plug into the port.
Sound (Line out ) Port Sound (Line Out/ Audio Out/ Sound Out ) - It is found on computer sound cards. It allows external speakers , headphones , or other output devices to be connected to the computer, transferring computer generated audio to the devices so that it can be heard .
SATA Port SATA Port - A Computer bus interface for connecting host bus adapters to mass storage devices such as hard disk drives and optical drives.
eSATA Port eSATA Port - In 2004 , the Serial ATA committee delivered the eSATA specification , which defines an external connector for the same SATA signalling conventions. This allows external powered hard drives to be attached to a PC's internal SATA controller.
Ethernet Port Ethernet Port (Also Known As Jack or Socket) - It is an opening on computer network equipment that Ethernet cables plug into.
Power Port Power Port - It allows electrical current to pass through the device for exclusive purposes of providing power to a device (not a data stream, for example, or anything more complex).
Serial Port Serial Port - In Computing a serial port is a serial communication interface through which information transfers in or our one bit at a time (in contrast to a parallel port).


Throughout most of the history of computers, data was transferred through serial ports to devices such as modems, terminals, and various peripherals.
Parallel Port Parallel Ports - It uses a 25-pin connector (type DB-25) and is used to connect printers, computers and other devices that need relatively high bandwidth. It is often called a Centronics interface after the company that designed the original standard for parallel communication between a computer and printer.
Games Port Games Port - It is a device port which was available in most personal computer - compatible systems. This input/output connector traditionally allowed users to connect game pads, joysticks or other compatible devices to provide additional functionality to the computer system.
S video Port S-video Port (Also known as separate video and Y/C) - It is a signaling standard for standard definition video, typically 480i or 576i. By separating the black and white and coloring signals, it achieves better image quality than composite video, but has lower color resolution than component video.
Optical Audio Port Optical Audio Port - It is a system that uses fiber optic cables and laser light to transmit digital audio signals between devices.
